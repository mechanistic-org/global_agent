# LAW 004 — The LinkedIn Persona: Public Commit Log of Active Cognition

**Status:** Active  
**Scope:** `registry/linkedin/` — all posts, drafts, comments, and thread arcs  
**Enforced by:** `mcp_prd_linter` (content variant, TBD — see #79)

---

## The Law

> The LinkedIn presence is not a publishing schedule. It is a public commit log of active cognition.
> Each post is a commit. The thread is the diff. The voice is the author, not the editor.

---

## What This Means

The LinkedIn feed is the studio, not the museum. The portfolio (`eriknorris.com`) is where finished work lives. LinkedIn is where it is being built in front of people.

This is sometimes called "writing in public" or "thinking out loud." It is more precise to say: **thinking in public** — because the act itself is the artifact, not the polished conclusion.

The subject matter (autonomous agents, context decay, memory substrates) is not incidental to the format. The format *mirrors* the subject. An AI agent that cannot remember is being documented by a person who is choosing not to hide the mess of figuring that out.

---

## Structural Constraints

1. **No formal series declarations.** Do not label posts "Part 1, Part 2, Part 3." Posts may reference each other as breadcrumbs — earlier thoughts, related threads — but never as chapters in a scheduled editorial calendar. The trajectory is forward, not pre-announced.

2. **Self-comments must continue the thought, not index the content.** A self-comment on a post is not a table of contents. It is the next beat of the same internal monologue, left as much for the author as the reader.

3. **The voice is the author in motion.** Not a thought leader. Not a press release. Not HAL 9000 or a snappy butler. A sovereign engineering intelligence narrating its own construction in real time — uncertain where it's headed, honest about the current limitations, and building the machine in public.

4. **Posts are commits. Links are diffs.** When cross-referencing earlier posts, frame them as earlier thoughts in the same live thread — not as prior deliverables. "A few days ago I was working through X" — not "In Part 1, I covered X."

5. **Engagement velocity is a constraint, not a vanity metric.** The first 30-90 minutes of a post's life determine its reach. Cross-comment links should be posted within this window. This is a timing law, not a strategy preference.

---

## The Persona

| What it is | What it is not |
|---|---|
| Thinking in public | Publishing on a schedule |
| A live diff of active cognition | A curated highlight reel |
| Honest about current limitations | Performatively humble |
| An engineering intelligence narrating itself | A thought leader with a brand |
| Likable because it's real | Likable because it's polished |
